Here's a detailed recipe for making Chole Bhature, a popular North Indian dish consisting of spicy chickpeas (chole) and deep-fried bread (bhature). Chole (Spicy Chickpeas) Ingredients: 2 cups chickpeas (soaked overnight) 2 onions, finely chopped 3 tomatoes, pureed 2 green chilies, chopped 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste 1 tsp cumin seeds 2 bay leaves 1 black cardamom 2-3 cloves 1-inch cinnamon stick 1 tsp turmeric powder 1 tsp red chili powder 1 tsp coriander powder 1 tsp cumin powder 1 tsp garam masala 1 tsp dry mango powder (amchur) 2 tbsp chole masala Salt to taste Fresh coriander leaves, chopped 2 tbsp oil Water as needed Instructions: Cooking the Chickpeas: Pressure cook the soaked chickpeas with water and a pinch of salt until they are soft (usually 4-5 whistles). Making the Masala: Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds, bay leaves, black cardamom, cloves, and cinnamon stick. Sauté until fragrant. Add the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Cook for another 2 minutes. Add the tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the masala. Adding Spices: Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt. Mix well. Add the cooked chickpeas along with the water they were boiled in. Stir to combine. Simmering: Add chole masala, garam masala, and amchur powder. Mix well and simmer on low heat for 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld. Finishing: Garnish with fresh coriander leaves before serving. Bhature (Deep-Fried Bread) Ingredients: 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida) 2 tbsp semolina (sooji) 1/2 cup yogurt 1 tsp sugar 1/2 tsp salt 1/2 tsp baking powder 1/4 tsp baking soda 2 tbsp oil Water as needed Oil for deep frying Instructions: Preparing the Dough: In a large bowl, mix together flour, semolina, sugar, salt, baking powder, and baking soda. Add yogurt and oil to the mixture and start kneading the dough. Add water gradually as needed to form a smooth and soft dough. Resting the Dough: Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 2 hours. Rolling the Bhature: Divide the dough into equal-sized balls. Roll each ball into a flat, round disc, about 5-6 inches in diameter. Frying: Heat oil in a deep frying pan. Fry each bhatura one at a time, pressing gently with a slotted spoon until it puffs up and turns golden brown on both sides. Remove from oil and drain on paper towels. Serving Serve the hot and fluffy bhature with the spicy chole, along with some sliced onions, green chilies, and lemon wedges on the side. Enjoy your delicious homemade Chole Bhature!
