Ingredients: For Pakoda: 1 cup gram flour (besan) 1 small onion, finely chopped 1 green chili, finely chopped 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder Salt to taste Water as needed Oil for frying For Kadhi: 1 cup yogurt 2 tablespoons gram flour (besan) 2 cups water 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder 1 teaspoon mustard seeds 1 teaspoon cumin seeds 1-2 dried red chilies 1 tablespoon chopped coriander leaves 1 tablespoon chopped curry leaves (optional) 2 tablespoons oil Salt to taste Instructions: Make Pakoras: In a mixing bowl, combine gram flour, chopped onions, green chili, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Gradually add water to form a thick batter. Heat oil in a deep pan for frying. Drop small portions of the batter into the hot oil and fry until golden brown. Remove the pakoras from the oil and drain excess oil on a paper towel. Prepare Kadhi: In a mixing bowl, whisk together yogurt, gram flour, turmeric powder, and water until smooth. Heat oil in a deep pan, add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and dried red chilies. Let them splutter. Pour the yogurt mixture into the pan and stir well. Add salt to taste and bring the mixture to a boil, stirring continuously. Reduce the heat and let the kadhi simmer for 15-20 minutes until it thickens slightly. Add the fried pakoras to the kadhi and cook for another 5-7 minutes. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and curry leaves. Serve: Serve hot with steamed rice or chapati. Enjoy your homemade Kadi Pakoda!
