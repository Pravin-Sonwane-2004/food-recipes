Ingredients For Misal Sprouts: 1 cup mixed sprouts (like moth beans, green gram) Spices & Masala: 1 large onion, finely chopped 2 tomatoes, finely chopped 1 green chili, chopped 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste 1/2 tsp mustard seeds 1/2 tsp cumin seeds 1/4 tsp asafoetida (hing) 1/2 tsp turmeric powder 1 tsp red chili powder 1 tsp garam masala 1 tsp goda masala (or garam masala if unavailable) 1 tsp coriander powder 1/2 tsp cumin powder Salt to taste Other: 1/2 cup tamarind pulp 2 tbsp oil 2 cups water For Kat (Spicy Gravy) 1 large onion, finely chopped 1 tomato, finely chopped 2 tbsp dry coconut, grated 1 tsp red chili powder 1/2 tsp turmeric powder 1/2 tsp garam masala 1/2 tsp goda masala Salt to taste 2 tbsp oil For Garnishing Farsan or sev Chopped onions Chopped coriander leaves Lemon wedges For Serving 8 pav (bread rolls) Instructions Preparing the Sprouts Cooking Sprouts: In a pressure cooker, add the sprouts, 2 cups of water, and a pinch of salt. Pressure cook for 1-2 whistles until the sprouts are soft. Set aside. Making the Misal Temper Spices: Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add cumin seeds, asafoetida, and curry leaves. Saute Vegetables: Add finely chopped onions and green chili. Saute until onions turn golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and saute until the raw smell disappears. Cook Tomatoes: Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and oil starts to separate. Add Spices: Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, goda masala, coriander powder, and cumin powder. Mix well and cook for a few minutes. Add Sprouts: Add the cooked sprouts along with their water. Add tamarind pulp and salt. Mix well and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes until the flavors meld together. Making the Kat (Spicy Gravy) Roast Coconut: In a separate pan, dry roast the grated coconut until it turns golden brown. Set aside. Saute Onions: In the same pan, heat oil and add chopped onions. Saute until they turn golden brown. Cook Tomatoes: Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft. Add Spices: Add red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, goda masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for a few minutes. Grind Mixture: Let the mixture cool and then grind it into a fine paste using a little water. Prepare Kat: Transfer the paste back to the pan. Add water to achieve a thin gravy consistency. Let it simmer for 10-15 minutes. Serving Assemble Misal Pav: In a serving bowl, add a portion of the misal. Pour some spicy kat (gravy) over it. Garnish: Top with farsan/sev, chopped onions, and coriander leaves. Serve with lemon wedges on the side. Serve Pav: Lightly toast the pav with a bit of butter on a pan until golden brown. Serve hot with the misal. Enjoy your delicious and spicy Misal Pav!
