Uttapa, also known as uttapam, is a popular South Indian breakfast dish. It's a type of dosa but thicker, similar to a pancake, and is often topped with vegetables. Here's a basic recipe to make uttapa at home: Ingredients: For the Batter: 1 cup rice (regular or parboiled) 1/3 cup urad dal (split black gram) 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi seeds) Salt to taste Water as needed For the Toppings: 1 onion, finely chopped 1 tomato, finely chopped 1 green chili, finely chopped 1 small carrot, grated (optional) Fresh coriander leaves, chopped Oil or ghee for cooking Instructions: Preparing the Batter: Soak Rice and Dal: Rinse the rice and urad dal separately under running water. Soak the rice with fenugreek seeds in one bowl and urad dal in another for about 4-6 hours. Grind the Batter: Drain the soaked rice and dal. Grind the urad dal with a little water to a smooth and fluffy batter. Grind the rice and fenugreek seeds to a slightly coarse paste. Combine both batters in a large bowl. Ferment the Batter: Add salt to the batter and mix well. Cover the bowl and let it ferment overnight or for 8-12 hours in a warm place. The batter should rise and become slightly bubbly. Making the Uttapa: Prepare the Toppings: Mix the chopped onion, tomato, green chili, carrot (if using), and coriander leaves in a bowl. Heat the Pan: Heat a non-stick tawa or griddle on medium heat. Lightly grease it with oil or ghee. Spread the Batter: Pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the pan and spread it gently to form a thick pancake, about 6-7 inches in diameter. Add Toppings: Quickly sprinkle the mixed vegetables evenly over the surface of the batter. Press the toppings gently with a spatula so they stick to the batter. Cook the Uttapa: Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges of the uttapa. Cook on medium heat until the bottom is golden brown and crisp. Flip the uttapa and cook the other side for another 1-2 minutes. Serve: Remove the uttapa from the pan and serve hot with chutney and sambar. Tips: The consistency of the batter should be thicker than dosa batter but still spreadable. You can add other vegetables like bell peppers, spinach, or corn for variation. Adjust the spiciness by adding or reducing green chilies. Enjoy your homemade uttapa!
