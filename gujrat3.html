Ingredients: 1 cup gram flour (besan) 1 cup yogurt 2 cups water 1 teaspoon ginger paste 1 teaspoon green chili paste 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder Salt to taste For tempering: 2 tablespoons oil 1 teaspoon mustard seeds 1 teaspoon sesame seeds 1 pinch asafoetida (hing) Few curry leaves 2-3 green chilies, slit lengthwise 2 tablespoons grated coconut 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves Instructions: Mixing the Batter: In a mixing bowl, combine gram flour, yogurt, water, ginger paste, green chili paste, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well to make a lump-free batter. Cooking the Batter: Transfer the batter to a non-stick pan. Cook the batter on low heat, stirring continuously to avoid lumps. Cook until the batter thickens and becomes smooth. It should take about 8-10 minutes. The batter should be of spreading consistency. Spreading the Mixture: Quickly spread a thin layer of the cooked batter onto the back of a greased plate or a clean kitchen counter. Use a flat spatula to spread the batter evenly. Rolling the Khandvi: Allow the spread batter to cool for a few minutes. Then, make cuts with a knife to form thin strips. Gently roll each strip into a tight roll. Repeat the process with all the strips. Preparing the Tempering: Heat oil in a small pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add sesame seeds, asafoetida, curry leaves, and green chilies. Saut√© for a few seconds. Tempering the Khandvi: Pour the tempering over the rolled khandvi rolls evenly. Garnishing: Garnish with grated coconut and chopped coriander leaves. Serving: Allow the khandvi to cool completely before serving. Enjoy your homemade khandvi!
